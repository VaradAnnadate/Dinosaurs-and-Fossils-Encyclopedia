Fossils

Fossils are the stone-like copies of ancient life, and they're our main window into the world of dinosaurs. It's important to know that these aren't the original bones! The most common process, called permineralization, begins when a dinosaur dies and is quickly buried by sediment. Over millions of years, water seeping through the ground dissolves the original bone and deposits minerals in its place, creating a perfect rock replica of the creature.

But fossils aren't just bones. Scientists also study trace fossils, which are the clues dinosaurs left behind. These include fossilized footprints that show how they walked or nests that show how they reproduced. Together, these rock-like clues are how paleontologists, the scientists who study fossils, piece together the story of these amazing ancient animals.
